	\hfill
	\parbox[t]{0.48\textwidth}{
		\tcolorbox[title=Abstract]
		Crystallographers describe unit cells using multiple mathematical spaces, each revealing different aspects of geometry, symmetry, or classification. Beginning with conventional parameters in \HVI{}, we trace transformations through \GVI{} and \SVI{} into visual (\PVI{}), geometric (DC7unsrt), and symbolic (\CIII{}) spaces.
		\endtcolorbox
	}
	
	\vspace{1cm}
	
	% Row 2 — HVI and GVI
	\noindent
	\parbox[t]{0.48\textwidth}{
		\tcolorbox[title=\HVI{} – Conventional Parameter Space]
		\textbf{\fontsize{65}{40}\selectfont Conventional Parameter Space (\HVI{})}\
		
		Six coordinates: $a$, $b$, $c$, $\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$. Not ideal for comparing geometry or symmetry directly.
		(``H'' was chosen to honor Ren\'e Just Ha\"uy.)
		\endtcolorbox
	}
	\hfill
	\parbox[t]{0.48\textwidth}{
		\tcolorbox[title=\GVI{} – Metric Tensor Space]
		\textbf{\fontsize{65}{40}\selectfont Metric Tensor Space (\GVI{})}
		
		Dot products of cell edges. Basis for Niggli reduction. Space is non-convex with complex boundary behavior.
		\endtcolorbox
	}
	
	\vspace{1cm}
	
	% Row 3 — SVI and PVI
	\noindent
	\parbox[t]{0.48\textwidth}{
		\tcolorbox[title=\SVI{} – Selling Scalar Space]
		\textbf{\fontsize{65}{40}\selectfont Selling Scalar Space (\SVI{})}\\
		Uses $d = -a - b - c$ transformation. Convex region; Bravais types form connected manifolds within.
		\endtcolorbox
	}
	\hfill
	\parbox[t]{0.48\textwidth}{
		\tcolorbox[

		\textbf{\fontsize{65}{30}\selectfont Projection Space (\PVI{})}\
		\
		
		Visual representation space in low dimensions. Highlights proximity between cells and symmetry transitions.
		\endtcolorbox
	}
	
	\vspace{1cm}
	
	% Row 4 — DC7 and Character Space
	\noindent
	\parbox[t]{0.48\textwidth}{
		\tcolorbox[title=DC7unsrt – Dirichlet Descriptor]
		\textbf{\fontsize{65}{40}\selectfont Dirichlet Descriptor (DC7unsrt)}\\
		Seven scalars derived from Voronoi cells. Focuses on geometric relationships and symmetry inference.
		\endtcolorbox
	}
	\hfill
	\parbox[t]{0.48\textwidth}{
		\tcolorbox[title=\CIII{} – Character Space]
		\textbf{\fontsize{65}{40}\selectfont Character Space (\CIII{})}\\
		Symbolic constraints (equalities, zeros, inequalities) form logical patterns that encode Bravais-type symmetries.
		\endtcolorbox
	}
